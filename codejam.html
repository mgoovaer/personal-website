Google Code Jam

Introduction
Since 2013 I have been participating in the yearly Google Code Jams. Simply put, they are online coding competitions organized by Google in which anyone can participate. The coding problems vary vastly in content, but the reoccuring theme is that they will test your ability to solve a problem and test that via provided input and submitted output.
I get such a rush from successfully completing coding problems that it's easy for me to participate. I have found that my biggest weakness is speed coding. We're not taught in my University courses to code quickly. Although it's a weakness, my participation in Code Jam has continually improved it.
2013
2013 was my first year participating in Code Jam, and I didn't know what to expect. I successfully completed both simple inputs for "Tic-Tac-Toe-Tomek" and "Fair and Square" within the Qualification Round. However, I just barely fell short of the 25 point cut off for advancement into the following round.
2014
Taking what I had learned from my participation in the 2013 Code Jam I prepared and took the 2014 Code Jam far more seriously. I successfully completed three out of the four problems ("Magic Trick", "Cookie Clicker Alpha", and "Deceitful War") advancing out of the Qualification Round with 55/90 points in the top 36%.
Round 1, however, was a different story. Even though I determinedly participated in all three iterations of the round (1A, 1B, and 1C) I was unable to beat the cut off for advancement into round 2. In round 1B, I did come up with a solution to the "New Lottery Game" for the small input.
The three hour time limit of these rounds was too short for me to successfully complete enough of these problems.
2015
I successfully completed two of the four problems within this past years qualification round and qualified for the next stage. However, due to other obligations I was unable to proceed with the competition.
